Samoyed

Samoyed's friendly nature makes them poor guard dogs. They are good companions, especially for little kids.Because of their cute smiling face, they also are called smiley dogs. When they are bored, they love diging things.

  • Breed: Samoyed
  • Life Span: 12-13 years
  • Weight: Female:16–20 kg, Male:20–30 kg
  • Origin: Northwest Russia and Western Siberia

Samoyeds are descended from the Nenets herding laika, a spitz-type dog from Siberia used for sledding, herding, guarding, and keeping their owners warm.Fridtjof Nansen believed that the use of sled dogs was the only effective way to explore the north and used Samoyeds on his polar expeditions. His plan to feed the weaker dogs to the stronger ones as the former died during the expedition ultimately consumed nearly all of his dogs.The Samoyed has been identified as a basal breed that predates the emergence of the modern breeds in the 19th Century.
